ARKRAY's Insulin Cool Pouch

According to ADA, although manufacturers recommend storage of insulin in the refrigerator, injection of cold insulin can sometimes make the injection more painful. Hence, many providers suggest storing the insulin ‘in-use’ at room temperature. To ensure quality, various organization such as ADA and ISPAD acknowledges that insulin is a temperature sensitive drug and should be protected from heat and freezing.

Optimal temperature control is needed especially for on-the-go consumers whereby bringing their ‘in-use’ insulin requires careful storage. With that, the Insulin cool pouch could provide an effective storage for ‘in-use’ insulin in a safe and effective manner. It is compact and easy to carry. Its temperature-control-technology can be activated by just immersing it in water.
